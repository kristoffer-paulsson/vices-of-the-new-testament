\item[Maleficent eye,]
\entlbl{maleficent eye} 

\grc{ὀφθαλμός πονηρός}
\index[grc]{οφθαλμος πονηρος@\grc{ὀφθαλμός πονηρός}} 
(\textit{ophthalmos ponēros}):
In TDNT, Michaelis denotes that \grc{ὀφθαλμός} means ``first `apple of the eye,' then `eye',''\bkfoot{\grc{ὀφθαλμός}}{5:375}{\tdntMichaelis{}} 
 further, \grc{πονηρός} means \emph{evil}, totally, \emph{apple of the maleficent eye}. See \entref{evil}, also \entref{maleficent contriver}, \entref{maleficent craving}, \entref{malevolence}, and \entref{malice}.
Found in Mark 7:22.
