\item[Idolatry,]
\entlbl{idolatry} 

\grc{εἰδωλολατρία}
\index[grc]{ειδωλολατρια@\grc{εἰδωλολατρία}} 
(\textit{eidōlolatria}):
According to Gingrich -- \emph{idolatry}. The sins cataloged in Col 3:5 -- \grc{πορνεία} (\emph{porneia}) \entref{prostitution}, \grc{ἀκαθαρσία}, (\emph{akatharsia}) \entref{foul}, \grc{πάθος} (\emph{pathos}) \entref{erotic perversion}, \grc{ἐπιθυμία κακός} (\emph{epithymia kakos}) \entref{maleficent craving}, \grc{πλεονεξία} (\emph{pleonexia}) \entref{unscrupulous encroaching} -- count as idolatry. Mentioned in 1~Cor 10:7--8 as part of idolatry: to eat \grc{ἐσθίω} (\emph{esthiō}); to drink \grc{πίνω} (\emph{pinō}); to amuse \grc{παίζω} (\emph{paizō}) erotically or festively; to prostitute someone or oneself \grc{πορνεύω} (\emph{porneuō}) understood as selling sex. Conduct mentioned in 1~Cor 10:18--21: to partner \grc{κοινωνός} (\emph{koinōnos}) with demons; to drink the cup \grc{ποτήριον} (\emph{potērion}) of demons; to eat from the table \grc{τράπεζα} (\emph{trapeza}) of demons. Thereby a rite, ceremony, or sacrifice. Also, see \entref{idol worship}, \entref{idol meat}, and \entref{idolater}.
Found in Gal 5:20; 1~Pet 4:3.
