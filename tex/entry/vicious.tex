\item[Vicious,]
\entlbl{vicious} 

\grc{ἀνήμερος}
\index[grc]{ανημερος@\grc{ἀνήμερος}} 
(\textit{anēmeros}):
Both Liddell, Thayer, and Gingrich agree in the meaning \emph{not tame}, \emph{wild}, and \emph{savage}. However, Danker proposes \emph{ferocious} and \emph{vicious}. \emph{Untamed} as ``left in a natural or wild state.''\cdfoot{untamed}{2023-03-11} And \emph{vicious} as ``(of an act) intending to hurt badly, or (of a person or animal) likely to be violent.''\cdfoot{vicious}{2023-03-11}
Found in 2~Tim 3:3.
