\item[Shameful talk,]
\entlbl{shameful talk} 

\grc{αἰσχρολογία}
\index[grc]{αισχρολογια@\grc{αἰσχρολογία}} 
(\textit{aischrologia}):
\newglossaryentry{aischrologia}
{
    name=\grc{αἰσχρολογία},
    description={\entrefgls{shameful talk}},
    sort=αισχρολογια@\grc{αἰσχρολογία}
}
According to Bultmann, the term is understood ``The rarer \emph{aischrotēs} [\entref{shameful}], which is attested only in Attic literature, is found once in Eph 5:4, where it occurs with \emph{mōrologia} [\entref{absurd talk}] and \emph{eutrapelia} [\entref{sexist speech}] in the sense of \emph{aischrologia}.''\bkfoot{\grc{αἰσχρολογία}}{1:191}{\tdntBultmann{}} 
Thereby, \grc{αἰσχρολογία} is a generic term for a group of words resembling \emph{absurd talk} and \emph{sexist speech}, because of that we explain them as shameful verbal conduct.
Found in Col 3:8.
